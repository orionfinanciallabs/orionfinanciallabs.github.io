How Home Loan EMI is Calculated?

Understanding your monthly commitment is crucial before signing the loan agreement. Here is a simple breakdown of how EMI works.

The EMI Formula

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is calculated using the following mathematical formula:

EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1]
  • P: Principal Loan Amount
  • R: Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)
  • N: Loan Tenure in Months

Example: Loan for a Flat in Borivali

If you take a loan of ₹1 Crore for 20 years at an interest rate of 7.50%:

  • Monthly EMI: ₹80,559
  • Total Interest Payable: ₹93,34,228
  • Total Amount (P + I): ₹1,93,34,228

Tip: Choosing a shorter tenure reduces your total interest burden significantly.
